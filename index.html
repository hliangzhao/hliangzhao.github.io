Biography

Research Interests

  • Learning-Augmented Algorithms & Systems. My research investigates how to integrate machine learning predictions into classic algorithmic frameworks to improve decision-making in uncertain and dynamic environments (see this preprint). Representative contributions include the algorithm PFSUM (NeurIPS '24) for the classic Bahncard problem, and a robustification framework Guard (arXiv: 2507.16242) for the online caching problem. These algorithms are designed not only for theoretical guarantees but also for real-world computer systems such as microservices platforms, MLSys, and heterogeneous job schedulers. I further explore embedding these techniques into system control loops to support predictive autoscaling, dynamic load dispatching, and low-latency request handling under highly non-stationary workloads.

  • Industrial Intelligence. I also work on intelligence systems for industrial and manufacturing applications, with a focus on multimodal data fusion, production task scheduling, and resource optimization. My research addresses how to unify temporal, structured, and perceptual signals from discrete manufacturing processes to enable adaptive, learning-driven decision making (see this slide). I explore constructing dynamic interaction graphs among machines, materials, and operators to support real-time scheduling and flow control in complex production environments. Additionally, I am investigating the development and deployment of vertical-domain foundation models for industrial scenarios such as fault detection, quality control, and equipment maintenance. These models aim to provide reliable semantics-aware decision support for high-throughput and resilient industrial systems.
